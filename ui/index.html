Exploratory analysis

What drives a customer to leave.

Five signals emerged from 100+ raw features as the strongest predictors of 60-day churn. Each one points to a distinct intervention strategy.

01
Usage decline is the leading signal.

The change in minutes-of-use over the trailing three months (change_mou) is the single strongest predictor. Churners' call volume falls at roughly three times the rate of customers who stay — a measurable drift that begins weeks before cancellation.

#1 Feature importance
02
Device age predicts upgrade intent.

45% of churners are on handsets that are one to two or more years old, compared to 30% of retained customers. Customers on aging devices are implicitly shopping for new hardware — often from a competing carrier.

45% Churners on old devices
03
Revenue decay tracks disengagement.

The declining revenue trend (change_rev) mirrors falling usage — customers gradually reduce their spend before they churn. This drift is detectable 4–6 weeks in advance if monitored weekly.

4–6w Early signal window
04
Value-for-money mismatch, not price.

A revenue-per-minute efficiency feature reveals that churners pay more per minute of actual use, not less. They receive less value from their plan — making targeted plan optimisation a more effective intervention than blanket discounts.

↑ rpm Churners' rev per min

"Churn is not a pricing problem. Churners and stayers pay nearly identical monthly amounts — $58.21 vs $59.22. Discounts alone will not retain them."

80,000 training / 20,000 test — stratified split

Evaluated on a stratified 80/20 train-test split — 20,000 held-out customers the model had never seen during training.

LightGBM's gradient-boosted tree structure captures the non-linear interaction between usage decline, device age, and billing patterns that a linear classifier cannot express.

The 50% base churn rate reflects the dataset's natural distribution — the model must discriminate effectively at random, making AUC-ROC the primary evaluation metric.

Real-world deployment

Built to run on your data.

The churn engine is not a research prototype. It is a production-ready scoring API designed to plug into any telecom CRM, billing pipeline, or data warehouse. Bring six fields from a subscriber record; receive a risk score, tier classification, and recommended action in under 50ms.

01
Daily batch scoring
Export your active subscriber base each morning and submit it to /predict/batch. The API accepts up to 1,000 customers per request and returns a risk-segmented list that feeds directly into your retention team's daily queue. High-risk customers are surfaced first.
02
Event-triggered scoring
Wire /predict into your billing or usage pipeline. When a customer's monthly minutes drop by more than 15%, or their device age crosses the 12-month mark, the event fires a score. The result routes automatically to the relevant tier — no analyst required.
03
Campaign budget allocation
Risk tiers translate directly to intervention cost. High-risk customers justify a dedicated account manager call ($40–60 per intervention). Medium-risk customers receive an automated loyalty offer ($5–10). Low-risk customers are left undisturbed. Precision targeting reduces waste.

Six fields. Any CRM.

No feature engineering required on your end. Submit a raw JSON object with these six standard subscriber metrics:

Field What it means Where to find it
months Subscriber tenure in months Account creation date
eqpdays Days since last device upgrade Device inventory system
rev_Mean Average monthly revenue over last 3 months Billing system
change_mou Change in monthly minutes vs 3-month average Usage records
change_rev Change in monthly revenue vs 3-month average Billing system
totmrc_Mean Average monthly recurring charge Billing system
Request — bash / curl
curl -X POST http://your-host:8000/predict \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "months": 18,
    "eqpdays": 480,
    "rev_Mean": 55.2,
    "change_mou": -120,
    "change_rev": -8.5,
    "totmrc_Mean": 42.0
  }'
Response — JSON
{
  "churn_probability": 0.74,
  "risk_tier": "High Risk",
  "recommended_action": "Priority outreach:
    dedicated account manager
    call, device upgrade offer,
    customised plan review."
}
